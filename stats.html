Career Stats

Kirk Cousins is a good quarterback as of the present, but he wasn’t always as good. Matter of fact, he was only a two-star recruit coming out of his private Christian school. In his first year of college, he wouldn’t even see a starting job. He would remain a backup to future NFL quarterback Brian Hoyer.

To the left are stats from Kirk’s cumulative high school years, college years, and overall career (bottom statline). As displayed in his career, he has a total of 270 touchdown passes with only 110 interceptions. That’s equivalent to almost 2.5 touchdowns per every singular interception.

High School Journey

Kirk Cousins attended Holland Christian High School in Holland, Michigan, where he played football as a quarterback. Despite not being heavily recruited, he showcased his skills and leadership on the field, earning a scholarship to Michigan State University.

Career Statistics

Throughout his career in the NFL, Kirk Cousins has established himself as a reliable quarterback with impressive statistics. As of the latest data, he has completed over 67% of his passes for more than 30,000 yards and 200 touchdowns.

His performance on the field has earned him multiple Pro Bowl selections and recognition as one of the top quarterbacks in the league.
